Carbonara

Carbonara is a Roman pasta dish made with eggs, pecorino cheese and guanciale.
Its signature rich and silky sauce comes from beaten eggs tossed with hot pasta.
The trick to making carbonara is making sure the pasta is hot enough to cook
the eggs,but not so hot that they curdle.

Ingredients

  • Guanciale: Buy guanciale (or cured pork cheek) at your local butcher.
    If you can't find it, you can substitute unsmoked bacon or pancetta.
  • Pasta: Recommended type of pasta are Spaghetti, Bucatini or Mezzemaniche.
  • Eggs: Usually one egg, plus one for every plate.
    So 2 eggs for 1 persone, 3 eggs for 2 peaople and so on.
  • Cheese: Hand-shredded Pecorino Romano cheese.
    You can substitute Parmesan, if preferred. Or use a mix of both.
  • Black pepper: A lot of black pepper powder.

Steps

  1. Put a large saucepan of water on to boil.
  2. Chop the guanciale, having first removed any rind.
  3. Whisk the eggs, 1/2 of the cheese, and some pepper in a bowl until smooth.
  4. When the water starts boiling add salt and add the pasta in it.
    Let the pasta cook for 1 or 2 min less then what indicated.
  5. Put the chopped guanciale on a frying pan, don't add anything to it. The guanciale will release its own fat in the pan.
    Let the guanciale cook until it is crisp, and take half of it out of the pan.
    Leave the rest in the pan setting the heat on low.
  6. When the pasta is ready, turn off the heat from the frying pan with the gunaciale in it.
    Then with a pasta fork or tongs put the pasta in the frying pan
    with the pancetta. Don't worry if a little water drops in the pan as well
    (you want this to happen) and don't throw the pasta water away yet.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy.
    If needed add a little bit of pasta cooking water to keep it saucy.
    You don't want it wet, just moist.
  8. Stir in the guanciale, then top with the remaining cheese and more black pepper.
